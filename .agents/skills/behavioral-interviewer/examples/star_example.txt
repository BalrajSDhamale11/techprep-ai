BEHAVIORAL INTERVIEW — STRONG vs WEAK ANSWER EXAMPLE
Question: "Tell me about a time you faced a technical challenge you couldn't solve immediately."

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════
WEAK ANSWER (Score: 2/5) — What NOT to accept
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════
"Once I had a bug in my code that I couldn't figure out. I Googled it for
a while and eventually fixed it. I learned that debugging takes patience."

WHY IT SCORES LOW:
- Situation: Completely vague — no project, no context, no stakes
- Task: Unclear what needed to be fixed or why it mattered
- Action: "Googled it" shows no systematic thinking
- Result: "Fixed it" gives no detail — no timeline, no impact
- Delivery: No specific detail to evaluate

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════
STRONG ANSWER (Score: 5/5) — What to reward
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════
"In my second year, I was building a student attendance system for our
college project. [SITUATION: specific context, real stakes]

My responsibility was the backend API — specifically a filter function
that was supposed to return attendance records for a date range.
[TASK: their specific role, not the team's role]

When I tested it, records from the last day of the range were always
missing. I spent 2 hours checking the SQL query, thinking it was an
off-by-one in the date comparison. I rewrote it three times. Nothing
worked. Eventually I added print statements at every step and found
the bug was actually in how Python's datetime library handles
timezone-naive vs timezone-aware objects — something I had never
encountered before. I fixed it by standardising all dates to UTC at
the point of input.
[ACTION: systematic, specific debugging process, shows thinking]

The filter worked correctly after that fix, and I documented the
timezone issue in our README so future team members would not hit
the same problem. It became the most-referenced section of our docs.
[RESULT: concrete outcome, impact beyond just fixing the bug, learning applied]"

WHY IT SCORES HIGH:
- Every STAR component is specific and easy to identify
- Uses "I" throughout — clear personal ownership
- Shows systematic debugging, not luck
- Result includes impact (documentation) not just "it worked"
- Appropriate length — detailed but not rambling

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════
COACHING NOTES
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════
- If a student has no work experience, ALWAYS tell them coursework projects count
- "My team" is a red flag — push them to describe THEIR specific contribution
- Vague results like "it went well" need a follow-up: "Can you quantify that?"
- Students often skip the Result — remind them it is the most important part
